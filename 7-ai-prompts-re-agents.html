Prompt #1 of 7
Listings & Marketing
BRANDLAB

Write a Listing Description
That Actually Sells the Home

Most listing descriptions read like a spec sheet. Buyers don't buy specs — they buy the feeling of living there. This prompt writes the story that sells.

🏠
Why This Matters
Your listing description is often the first impression before a showing is ever scheduled. A compelling description increases showing requests and justifies the price before a buyer walks through the door. Most agents spend 10 minutes on this. You'll spend 3 — and get a better result.
Copy This Prompt →
You are an expert real estate copywriter who specializes in writing emotionally compelling listing descriptions that generate showing requests and attract serious buyers.

Write a listing description for a home with these details:
- [Bedrooms] bed / [Bathrooms] bath · [Square footage] sq ft
- Location: [City/Neighborhood]
- Top 5 features: [List your best features here]
- Target buyer: [e.g., young family, downsizer, investor]
- Asking price: [$XXX,XXX]

Write three versions: (1) Short — 75 words for MLS, (2) Medium — 150 words for Zillow/Realtor.com, (3) Long — 250 words for the property website. Lead each version with an emotional hook, not a feature list. Use present tense. Make the buyer feel like they're already living there.
3
Minutes to Use
Paste, swap your details, get 3 versions instantly
3
Versions Generated
MLS, Zillow/Realtor, and full website copy in one shot
↑
Outcome
More showings, faster offers, price-justified marketing
Pro Tip from Dionne
After you get the output, ask the AI: "Now rewrite the hook using the #1 emotional benefit for a [target buyer type]." The second version is almost always better than the first.
🎯
What This Gets You
A professional, emotionally compelling listing description in under 5 minutes — ready for MLS, Zillow, and your property site — without hiring a copywriter.

Prompt #2 of 7
Scripts & Objections
BRANDLAB

Handle Any Objection
Confidently — Every Time

"I want to wait until the market improves." "Your commission is too high." "I need to talk to my spouse first." Sound familiar? This prompt prepares you for every one of them.

💬
Why This Matters
The difference between agents who close consistently and those who don't often comes down to one thing: what they say when a prospect pushes back. Most agents freeze or default to discounting. This prompt gives you three professional, honest responses to any objection — instantly.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a real estate sales coach with 30+ years of experience training top-producing agents. A prospect just said:

"[Paste the exact objection here]"

I am a real estate agent at [Your Brokerage Name] in [City/Market]. My average days on market is [X days] and my list-to-sale ratio is [X%].

Give me 3 different responses to this objection: (1) a direct and confident response, (2) an empathetic, curiosity-based response, and (3) a story-based response using a past client situation. Each response should be 3–5 sentences, conversational, non-pushy, and rooted in the client's best interest — not a commission close.
Pro Tip from Dionne
Run this prompt for your top 5 most common objections before your next listing appointment. Print them out. Read them twice. You'll walk in prepared for anything they throw at you — and your confidence will show.
🏆
What This Gets You
Three professionally crafted, honest objection responses for any pushback — ready before every appointment so you never freeze or stumble in the moment.

Prompt #3 of 7
Social Media & Content
BRANDLAB

A Week of Social Content
in Under 10 Minutes

Consistency is what builds trust on social. Most agents post when they remember. This prompt gives you a full week of on-brand, value-forward content in one sitting.

📱
Why This Matters
Agents who post consistently are the ones people think of when they're ready to buy or sell. You don't need to go viral — you need to stay visible. A week of content in 10 minutes means no more blank-page paralysis or skipping posting because you're busy.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a social media strategist specializing in personal branding for real estate agents. Create a 7-day content calendar for a real estate agent with this profile:

- Name: [Your Name] · Market: [City/Neighborhood]
- Specialty: [e.g., first-time buyers, luxury, investors, relocation]
- Personality: [e.g., direct and no-nonsense / warm and community-focused / educational and data-driven]
- Current focus: [e.g., getting listings / building my buyer pipeline / growing my referral network]

For each day, provide: the post type (tip, story, market update, behind-the-scenes, testimonial, question, or educational), the full caption with hashtags for Instagram/Facebook, and a LinkedIn version (more professional tone). Every post should position me as the local expert — never generic, always specific to my market.
Pro Tip from Dionne
After you get the week of content, ask: "Now give me 5 story-style posts based on real client situations without using client names." Those perform 3× better than any market update.
📣
What This Gets You
A full 7-day content calendar with captions for Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn — written in your voice and specific to your market. Done in one sitting.

Prompt #4 of 7
Buyer Consultations
BRANDLAB

Walk Into Every Buyer Consultation
Fully Prepared

A great consultation doesn't happen by showing up and winging it. This prompt builds you a custom preparation sheet for every buyer meeting — in 4 minutes flat.

🤝
Why This Matters
Buyers choose their agent in the first meeting — often in the first 10 minutes. Walking in with specific market knowledge, pre-prepared questions, and a clear value proposition separates you from every agent who just shows homes. This prompt makes you that agent every time.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a real estate consultant coach. Prepare me for a buyer consultation meeting with a prospect who has the following profile:

- Budget: [$XXX,XXX – $XXX,XXX]
- Timeline: [e.g., 60–90 days]
- Looking in: [Neighborhood/City]
- Situation: [e.g., first-time buyer, relocating from out of state, upsizing from a condo]
- How they found me: [e.g., referral from John Smith, saw my Instagram, open house]

Provide: (1) 5 discovery questions to ask early to understand their real motivation, (2) 3 potential concerns this type of buyer typically has and how to address them, (3) a clear value proposition script (90 seconds) explaining why working with me exclusively is in their best interest, and (4) 3 current market facts I should know for this buyer's search criteria to demonstrate local expertise.
Pro Tip from Dionne
Run this prompt the night before every consultation. Print the output and review it in your car for 5 minutes before you walk in. The confidence that comes from being prepared is something buyers can feel — and it's why they sign with you.
✅
What This Gets You
A custom consultation prep sheet with targeted discovery questions, objection prep, a 90-second value pitch, and market facts — specific to each buyer — in under 5 minutes.

Prompt #5 of 7
Database & Sphere of Influence
BRANDLAB

Re-Activate Your Database
Without Being Annoying

Your database is your most valuable asset — and most agents barely touch it. This prompt writes personal, non-salesy re-engagement messages that actually get replies.

📋
Why This Matters
The agents who consistently earn referrals are not the ones who are the "best" agents — they're the ones who stay top of mind without being annoying about it. One personal message to your database this week could generate a listing appointment this month. This prompt writes it for you.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a relationship marketing expert specializing in real estate. Write a personal re-engagement message for a past client or sphere contact with this context:

- Contact name: [First Name]
- How I know them: [e.g., helped them buy their home 3 years ago / neighborhood friend / former colleague]
- Last time we spoke: [e.g., roughly 18 months ago / at a holiday party / after their closing]
- Something personal I remember: [e.g., they have two kids, love hiking, just retired]
- My goal: [e.g., stay top of mind / offer a home value update / reconnect naturally]

Write: (1) a text message (under 160 characters, warm and human), (2) an email (3–4 sentences, no sales language, genuine curiosity), and (3) a voicemail script (30 seconds, conversational). None of them should feel like a marketing message. They should feel like a friend checking in.
Pro Tip from Dionne
Run this for 5 contacts every Monday morning. That's 20 touches per month to your database with zero extra effort. In 90 days, you'll see the referrals come back. I promise — your database never forgets an agent who stayed in touch.
💌
What This Gets You
A personalized text, email, and voicemail script for each database contact — written to feel human, not like a drip campaign — so your sphere remembers you when someone asks for an agent recommendation.

Prompt #6 of 7
Pricing Strategy
BRANDLAB

Have the Price Reduction Conversation
Without Losing the Listing

This is the conversation most agents dread. Handled well, it deepens trust. Handled poorly, it costs you the listing. This prompt prepares you for both outcomes.

📉
Why This Matters
Overpriced listings sit. Sitting listings get stigmatized. Stigmatized listings either expire or sell for less than they would have if priced correctly from day one. The ability to have an honest pricing conversation — with data, empathy, and confidence — is one of the highest-value skills in real estate.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a real estate pricing expert and client communication coach. I need to have a price reduction conversation with a seller. Here is the situation:

- Property: [Address/Type] listed at [$XXX,XXX]
- Days on market: [X days]
- Showings: [X showings, X feedback received]
- Feedback themes: [e.g., "price too high for the updates," "location concern," "compared to X address"]
- Comparable sold prices: [Recent comps in the area]
- Seller's emotional state: [e.g., anxious, defensive, motivated, unrealistic]

Provide: (1) a data presentation script using the showing and comp data to frame the conversation, (2) an empathy bridge — words to acknowledge their feelings without validating an overpriced mindset, and (3) a specific recommendation script with the new price and the logic behind it that makes the case without being adversarial.
Pro Tip from Dionne
The best version of this conversation starts with: "The market is giving us feedback — and I want us to respond to it together." You are not the bad news. The market is. Let AI help you say it right.
📊
What This Gets You
A data-backed, empathy-led price reduction script tailored to your specific seller situation — so you walk in confident, not dreading the call.

Prompt #7 of 7
Lead Conversion
BRANDLAB

Turn Open House Visitors into
Clients Within 48 Hours

You collected their names. Most agents send a generic "thanks for coming." This prompt turns every open house visitor into a personalized, timed follow-up sequence that converts.

🏡
Why This Matters
Open houses are one of the highest-leverage lead generation activities in real estate — and most agents leave 90% of those leads on the table with a weak follow-up. Speed, personalization, and a clear next step are what separate the agents who convert from those who just host.
Copy This Prompt →
You are a real estate lead conversion specialist. A prospect visited my open house and I need a personalized follow-up sequence. Here are the details:

- Visitor name: [Name]
- Interest level noted: [e.g., very interested, just browsing, seemed motivated]
- Notes from conversation: [e.g., mentioned looking for 90 days, has a home to sell first, loved the kitchen, concerned about the school district]
- Property they visited: [Address — $XXX,XXX — X bed/X bath]
- Did they give me their contact info: [Yes — text/email / No — only signed in]

Write a 3-touch follow-up sequence: Touch 1 — same-day text (warm, personal, references something specific from our conversation). Touch 2 — next-day email (provides value, references their specific situation, includes a soft CTA). Touch 3 — Day 3 text or call script (checks in, gauges interest, suggests a next step). Every message should feel like it was written just for them — because it was.
Pro Tip from Dionne
Run this prompt for every open house visitor within 2 hours of the event ending. Agents who follow up same-day convert at 3× the rate of those who wait until Monday. Speed is respect. Use AI so you're always fast.
🚀
What This Gets You
A personalized 3-touch follow-up sequence — same-day text, next-day email, and Day 3 check-in — that turns open house visitors into booked appointments without generic drip campaigns.
